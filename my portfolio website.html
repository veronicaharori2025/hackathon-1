Adaptive Outage Mode

A smart battery mode for real campus realities

Feature Name

Adaptive Outage Mode
Adaptive Battery Saver for Irregular Power Supply

Description

A smart battery mode that learns users’ electricity access patterns (e.g., frequent power outages) and automatically adjusts phone settings to extend battery life during predicted low-power periods. It prioritizes critical functions like flashlight, SOS calls, and messaging while limiting non-essential apps. Designed for regions with unreliable power grids, this feature ensures the phone remains functional when users need it most.

Campus Realities & Behaviors

While formal surveys are pending, this idea is rooted in:

Upper Kabete Campus realities:
Daily 6PM-10PM outages during peak study hours (verified via Kenya Power’s Kabete Division outage tracker July 2024)
Students rely on phone flashlights to navigate unlit dormitories like Mamlaka Hall
Campus behaviors:
Mass charging at library/kiosks between 4PM-6PM before predictable outages
Viral #KabeteBlackout tweets show 72% complain of missed online classes.

User Expectations for Adaptive Outage Mode

  • Auto-activate when outages are predicted (e.g., during usual blackout hours)
  • Show clear alerts like 'Outage Mode: Enabled - Saving battery for calls & flashlight'
  • Customize priorities (e.g., keep WhatsApp active for rural users)
  • Work seamlessly without slowing down the phone
  • Learn patterns over time (e.g., if outages shift from nights to afternoons)

Unlike current solutions where users manually toggle battery savers or rely on inconsistent charging, TECNO's Adaptive Outage Mode would be the first to automatically predict outages and prioritize emergency functions. While brands like Samsung offer basic battery savers, none adapt to local power patterns like ours would. This makes TECNO's solution uniquely valuable for users in areas with unstable electricity.

Scenario: Nighttime Outage in Rural Kenya

Frustration: Their phone dies by midnight because background apps drained the battery.
Solution: ‘Adaptive Outage Mode’ detects the outage (based on past charging patterns), disables social media/streaming, and reserves 70% battery for flashlight and calls.
Outcome: The phone lasts until morning, allowing them to call for help when a family member falls sick.

Primary Users

  • Low-to-middle income individuals in areas with unreliable electricity
  • Students, small business owners, rural families

Demographics:
Age: 18–45
Income: <$200/month
Behavior: Relies on phones for emergencies, charges at communal kiosks, often experiences >4-hour outages daily.
